## Design Context

### Users
NUS ISS capstone committee, supervisors, and peers viewing a live demo presentation of the Twinkl VIF pipeline. Single reviewer at a time, usually over the presenter's shoulder on a laptop or projected screen. The goal is to quickly convey that the system is real, rigorous, and thoughtfully built — not a toy prototype.

### Brand Personality
Warm, analytical, rigorous. Three words: **compass · evidence · depth**.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Light mode only
- Scholarly-analytical: like a well-designed research tool or data notebook, not a SaaS product
- Warm parchment tones (existing palette is good — retain the terracotta/forest/navy semantic colours)
- Typography-forward: the serif font (Iowan Old Style) is correct and distinctive; lean into it
- Anti-references: no SaaS dashboard chrome, no hero metric cards with big numbers, no card-grid monoculture, no side-stripe accents on alerts

### Layout Principles
- The 3-column always-on layout is too rigid for a demo. Prefer a focused, vertical narrative flow: persona selection at top, then a collapsible journal section, then results below.
- Journal timeline should be hidden by default and toggled open — it's reference material, not the primary story.
- Results (critic scores, drift, digest) are the centrepiece and should breathe.

### Design Principles
1. **Evidence over chrome** — every visual element should either show data or help navigate to it. Decoration that doesn't carry information should be removed.
2. **Narrative flow** — the layout should guide the eye through the story: who is this persona → what did they write → what does the critic say → what drifted.
3. **Warm restraint** — the palette is earthy and warm; don't fight it. But strip gratuitous gradients and shadows that make it feel generic.
4. **Typography does the work** — hierarchy via size/weight/spacing, not boxes and borders.
5. **Data is legible** — charts and tables have enough breathing room to be read at a glance during a presentation.
