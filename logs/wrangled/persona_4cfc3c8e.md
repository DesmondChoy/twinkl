## Entry 3 - 2025-09-09

Charge nurse Fatima made another comment today about my "lifestyle." We were in the break room and she was talking about how she works double shifts to save for her daughter's education, and then she looked at me and said "some of us have responsibilities." As if I don't work hard. As if nursing isn't exhausting whether you stay for eight hours or sixteen.

I don't understand why taking care of myself is seen as selfishness. I give everything to my patients when I'm on shift. I held that mother's hand for three hours on Tuesday. I stayed late to comfort a seven-year-old who was scared of his IV. But because I don't sacrifice my entire existence to work, I'm somehow less committed?

My father worked himself into a heart attack at fifty-two. I watched him in that hospital bed, gray and diminished, and I swore I would never let a job steal my life from me. Fatima doesn't know that. Nobody at work knows that. They just see a young woman who goes to the beach and assume she doesn't take anything seriously.

**Nudge:** "What was it like watching your father in that hospital bed?"

**Response:** Terrifying. He looked so small. He was always the strongest person I knew, the one who fixed everything, and suddenly he couldn't even lift a glass of water. I remember thinking: this is what happens when you give everything to work and keep nothing for yourself. I was nineteen. I had just started nursing school. I promised myself I would never end up like that.
