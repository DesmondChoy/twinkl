## Entry 3 - 2026-01-05

Had a moment at work that actually felt good for once. Jenkins pipeline broke around ten and half the team was panicking because the release was supposed to go out by noon. I've seen this exact failure pattern maybe four times in the past three years — something with the artifact caching layer timing out when the build queue backs up.

I didn't rush. I pulled up the logs, found the timeout, bumped the threshold, re-triggered the build. Whole thing was fixed in twenty minutes. One of the junior devs asked how I knew where to look and I just said I'd been around long enough. Which is true. Twenty-two years at the same shop means you've seen every way the plumbing can break.

Driving home I felt something I don't feel that often anymore — like I was exactly where I should be. Not buzzing, not bored, just fitted into the right slot. Linda asked how my day was and I said fine, but it was more than fine. It was one of those days where the years of showing up actually meant something concrete.
