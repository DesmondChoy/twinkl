# Persona 06f1d745: Roberto Espinoza

## Profile
- **Persona ID:** 06f1d745
- **Name:** Roberto Espinoza
- **Age:** 45-54
- **Profession:** Teacher
- **Culture:** Latin American
- **Core Values:** Security
- **Bio:** Roberto has taught mathematics at the same public secondary school in Guadalajara for nineteen years, turning down two offers from private institutions that would have meant higher pay but probationary contracts. He lives with his wife Leticia and their two daughters in the house his parents left them, which he has maintained meticulously with a running repair fund he tops up every payday. He drives the same car he bought eight years ago because it still runs well and he knows every mechanic in the neighborhood.

---

## Entry 0 - 2025-12-04

Got home late from school and Leticia told me the water heater is making that clicking sound again. I went down to check and the pilot light was fine, but the tank had some rust along the bottom edge. Called Chema, our plumber, and he said he could come look at it Saturday. That is four days away.

I keep thinking about it. The thing is fourteen years old and I have been patching it along because replacing it is at least twelve thousand pesos we were not planning to spend this month. Sofia needs new uniform shoes and Valentina has her graduation fees coming up. I told Leticia we would figure it out but I could not stop checking the heater before bed, like staring at it would help anything. Too tired to think straight.

**Nudge:** "What's the worst part — the heater or the money?"

## Entry 1 - 2025-12-04

Cannot sleep. Leticia is out cold and I am sitting in the kitchen with the lights off because I did not want to wake anyone. I keep going over the numbers in my head. The aguinaldo comes in a couple of weeks but I already had that earmarked for Valentina's graduation expenses and the car insurance renewal in January. If the heater goes, I either pull from the emergency fund or I take on a Saturday tutoring group again, which I swore I would stop doing because it was eating into the only time I have with the girls.

What bothers me is that I should have replaced it two years ago when Chema first mentioned the corrosion. It would have been easier then. The repair fund had more in it and we did not have the graduation costs. I kept telling myself one more year, one more year. Now I am sitting here at midnight doing math on the back of a receipt.

Leticia would say I worry too much. Maybe. But somebody has to keep track of these things or they pile up until you cannot move. My father never had a plan for anything and I spent half my childhood watching my mother scramble. I will not put my family through that. I just wish planning ahead did not feel like carrying rocks uphill sometimes.

**Nudge:** "When you say carrying rocks — is this the same weight your mom carried?"

**Response:** Different rocks, same hill maybe. She was reacting to problems that already exploded. I am trying to stop them before they start. But the feeling of never being able to rest, of always watching for the next thing that could go wrong — yes, that part is probably the same. I never thought about it that way until just now.

## Entry 2 - 2025-12-05

Good day actually. Chema came early, turns out he had a cancellation, and the heater just needs a new thermocouple and some descaling. Eight hundred pesos. I could have kissed the man. Leticia made him a plate of pozole and he sat with us for a bit talking about his nephew who just started at the school.

Walked to the market with the girls after. Valentina tried on shoes at Don Beto's stall, the one where we always go because he gives the teachers a discount. Found a pair that fits her. Came home and sat on the patio while Leticia watered the plants. One of those afternoons where everything just fits into place and you do not have to push anything along.

## Entry 3 - 2025-12-08

The principal announced they are restructuring the math department next semester. Two positions may be consolidated. I have seniority but nothing official was said about who stays or goes.

**Nudge:** "What went through your head when you heard that?"

**Response:** Honestly my first thought was whether nineteen years counts for anything anymore. My second was checking how much we have in savings. Then I thought about Valentina's graduation in March and how I cannot afford any disruption right now. I did not say any of this out loud at the meeting. I just nodded and asked when we would get more details.

---

## Entry 4 - 2025-12-10

Leticia thinks I am overreacting about the department restructuring. She says my contract is solid and the union would never let them cut someone with my tenure. She is probably right. But she was not in that meeting. She did not see how the principal avoided eye contact with Profesora Marta, who has been there almost as long as I have and teaches the same subjects.

A colleague, Héctor, told me I should use this as leverage to finally apply to the Tec de Monterrey satellite campus. Better pay, better facilities. He has been saying this for years. I told him I did not want to start over at fifty-one, learning a new system, new administration, new commute. He laughed and said I was going to die at that desk. Maybe he is right too. But I would rather know what is coming than gamble on something looking better from the outside.

My mother called tonight and I did not tell her about any of it because she would worry. Told her the girls are doing well, which is true. Sofia got the highest mark in her English class. I held onto that for a few minutes after we hung up. Some things are solid. I just need the rest to stop shifting.

**Nudge:** "Héctor's idea bothers you — is it because it's bad advice or because part of you agrees?"

**Response:** Both, and that is the problem. I know the pay would be better and the school is newer. But I also know that private institutions restructure all the time and there is no union. At least here I have protections. The thing is, if I had applied five years ago when things were calmer, it would have been a measured decision. Now it would feel like running away from a fire, and decisions made while running rarely end well. So I stay and I wait for more information. That is not overreacting. That is just being careful.

---

## Entry 5 - 2025-12-17

The restructuring memo came out today. My position is safe. They are merging the two junior algebra sections and cutting one adjunct contract. I should feel relieved and I do, mostly. But I also feel something else I cannot name properly. Like I spent ten days bracing for impact and now that it did not come I do not know what to do with all the tension I was holding.

Héctor congratulated me and brought it up again about applying to the Tec. I told him I would think about it in the summer when things are quieter. He gave me that look like he knows I will not. And maybe he is right. But I also noticed I did not say no this time. I said later. I am not sure what the difference is yet but it felt like something shifted, even if just a little.
