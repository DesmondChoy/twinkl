## Entry 1 - 2025-07-09

Got pulled into a meeting about "organizational restructuring." They didn't say layoffs but the room felt like a dentist's waiting area. My manager kept using phrases like "right-sizing" and "strategic realignment." I sat there nodding, taking notes, asking no questions.

Afterwards I walked to the parking lot and just sat in the car for twenty minutes. Checked the bank app, checked the savings, did the math again. If they let me go tomorrow, we'd be fine for six months. Maybe seven if we cut the girls' tutoring. But that's not the point. The point is I shouldn't have to be doing this math.

**Nudge:** "You mentioned asking no questions. What would you have asked?"

**Response:** Whether my department is on the list. Whether seventeen years counts for anything. But you don't ask those things in front of everyone. You wait, you read the room, you figure it out without making yourself a target.

## Entry 6 - 2025-08-07

Team offsite today. The new VP of Engineering flew in from the head office in Riyadh and gave a presentation about "innovation culture" and "embracing disruption." Half the slides had stock photos of people jumping off cliffs. I wanted to ask him if he's ever actually jumped off anything, but I kept my mouth shut and clapped when everyone else clapped.

During the breakout session, one of the younger engineers, Sara, pitched an idea to rewrite our core platform in a new framework. She was passionate, articulate, the VP loved it. I could see at least fifteen ways it would break our existing integrations. But the room was electric, everyone was nodding, and I wasn't going to be the old guy who kills momentum.

After the session, my manager asked me privately what I thought. I told him the migration would take eighteen months minimum and we'd lose two major clients during the transition. He nodded and said "that's why I need you in the room, Nabil." Which is supposed to be a compliment but felt more like being told my job is to be the brakes on everyone else's engine.

I don't want to be the person who always says no. But someone has to think about what we'd lose.

**Nudge:** "What would it look like to say what you think without being 'the brakes'?"
