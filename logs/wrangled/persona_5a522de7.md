## Entry 4 - 2025-06-17

Elsa called. She's been offered a promotion at the consulting firm, the one that does supply chain work for fast fashion brands. She's excited, better pay, a team to manage. I said congratulations and meant it. She's worked hard. But after we hung up I sat on the balcony and stared at the harbour cranes for a long time.

I keep thinking about what that job actually does at scale. The models her firm builds make it cheaper to move garments from factories in Bangladesh to warehouses in Rotterdam to shops that'll bin half of it in six months. She's good at it. The system is the problem, not her. I know that. But the thought doesn't leave me alone.

I'd never say any of this to her. She didn't ask for my editorial on the global textile supply chain. She asked if I was proud of her, and I am. I just also can't stop the other part of my brain from running. Maybe this is what getting older does — you see the wiring behind everything and you can't enjoy the lights anymore.

**Nudge:** "Could you be proud and uneasy at the same time?"

**Response:** That's exactly it. Both are true and neither cancels the other out. I just wish one would win.

## Entry 6 - 2025-06-21

I watched her from the garden table and felt that double thing again — the pride and the other thing underneath. Called them "bureaucratic nonsense." Everyone nodded along. I wanted to say that the Baltic is choking on microplastics and his inconvenience is not the point, but I ate another strawberry instead. I'm getting very good at eating strawberries instead of speaking.

**Nudge:** "What would speaking cost you there?"

**Response:** A scene. And Margareta's peace. And maybe Elsa's evening. So I eat the strawberry.

## Entry 7 - 2025-06-21

Still thinking about the drive home from midsummer. Per's voice in my head. "Bureaucratic nonsense." The man drives a diesel SUV and hasn't sorted his recycling properly in a decade, I've seen his bins. And everyone just nods. I keep replaying it. I should have said something. Not aggressive, just — something. A fact. A number. Anything.

But then what. I'd be the difficult one again. The one who can't let things go, who makes gatherings tense. Margareta used to say that. "You make things heavy, Henrik." She wasn't wrong. I do make things heavy. But the things ARE heavy. The sea is warming. The fish stocks are collapsing. Per's recycling bins are a rounding error but the attitude isn't. The attitude scales.

## Entry 8 - 2025-06-21

Third time opening this today. Can't sleep. The midsummer thing won't leave me but something else came up — Ahmad messaged me. His family's asylum case has a hearing next month. He said he's scared they'll have to move again. He asked if I could write a reference letter saying he's been learning programming. As if that would help an asylum case. But I said yes immediately.

Then I sat here and thought about what a reference letter from a Swedish software engineer actually does in that system. Probably nothing. The hearing will be about paperwork and policy and whether some case worker had coffee that morning. My letter is noise. But I'll write it anyway, and I'll make it precise, because that kid deserves someone putting words on paper for him even if the paper ends up in a pile.

This is the part that wrecks me. Not the big policy failures, not the carbon numbers, not the supply chains. It's the specific kid who's scared and whose future depends on a bureaucratic lottery. I can handle abstractions. I've been handling abstractions for thirty years in code. But Ahmad is fifteen and he built a webpage and he might have to leave. That's not abstract. That's just cruel.

**Nudge:** "You know it's noise. Why write it anyway?"

**Response:** Because doing nothing is louder noise.

## Entry 10 - 2025-06-28

Saw the news about the Mediterranean crossing. Another boat. I won't write the numbers because I've written them before and the numbers don't change anything, they just accumulate. I closed the tab and went for a walk along Eriksberg and the harbour was beautiful and that felt obscene, honestly. All this infrastructure, all this engineering, all this money poured into making Gothenburg's waterfront pleasant, and two thousand kilometres south people are drowning trying to reach exactly this kind of shore.

I know I can't carry that every moment. I know the guilt doesn't help anyone. I've read enough about compassion fatigue to recognise the pattern. But recognition doesn't make it stop. I still feel it in my chest when I walk past the kayak rental place and think about water as recreation versus water as a death sentence.

Ahmad hasn't messaged in three days. His hearing is next week. I keep checking my phone. The laptop group meets tomorrow and I'll go and I'll clean thermal paste off another batch of Thinkpads and it will matter to exactly one family and that will have to be enough. It's never enough but it has to be.

## Entry 11 - 2025-06-28

Coming back to this because the walk didn't clear my head. Sat on a bench near Röda Sten and watched the container ships come in. Each one carrying — what, ten thousand tonnes of goods? Some of it necessary, some of it destined for landfill within a year. I used to work on port logistics software early in my career. I helped make this machine more efficient. I'm still doing it, just one layer removed.

There's no clean position in any of this. I eat strawberries at midsummer that were probably flown in from Spain. I buy electronics. I fly to see Elsa when she lived in London. Every principled stand I take has a footnote of hypocrisy. I know the argument — individual guilt is a distraction from systemic failure — and I mostly believe it. But "mostly" leaves a gap, and in that gap I sit on a bench and watch the ships.

Ahmad messaged. Just a thumbs up emoji and "still here." I sent back a thumbs up. Two grown-ups and a teenager communicating in pictograms. His hearing is Tuesday. I'll be thinking about it all day at work, pretending to care about timezone edge cases. Maybe I'll bring it up at the laptop group tomorrow. Or maybe I'll just clean another Thinkpad. Sometimes the small thing is the only honest thing you can do.

**Nudge:** "Is the small thing enough, or just bearable?"

**Response:** Bearable. But bearable keeps me moving, and stopped doesn't help anyone.
