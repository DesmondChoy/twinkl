## Entry 3 - 2025-01-09

Got an email today from a former colleague, Deepa, about a senior position opening at the water authority back in Chennai. Permanent role, government pension, the whole thing. She said she thought of me immediately. My mother would cry with happiness if I took it. Appa would finally stop asking pointed questions about "what the plan is." Arjun's parents would stop whispering about how their son married a woman who can't settle down.

I sat with the email open for maybe twenty minutes. I could picture the whole life — the flat in Adyar, Meera going to the same school I went to, weekend lunches at my parents' house. Predictable. Comfortable. Everything mapped out for the next thirty years. And I felt this thing in my chest, not panic exactly but something close. Like the walls of that life were already closing in even though I haven't said yes to anything. I know the stipend is barely enough. I know Arjun is tired of being the only one earning properly. I know my savings are basically gone. I know all of this.

But I closed the email and went back to my data. I'll probably tell Deepa thanks but not now, maybe later, which is what I always say. The part I can't explain to anyone is that the uncertainty here feels more honest than the certainty there. That sounds ridiculous when I say it out loud but there it is.

**Nudge:** "What does 'honest' mean here — honest about what?"

**Response:** About what I actually want, I think. The Chennai job would be fine. Good, even. But it would be someone else's version of my life. The one everyone expected me to have. Here I'm struggling but at least the struggle is mine.
