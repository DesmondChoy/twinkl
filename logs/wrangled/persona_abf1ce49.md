## Entry 0 - 2025-09-07

First full week done at Birch Lane. The kids are sweet. One boy, Mateo, already told me he wants to read "chapter books like his sister." That kind of thing is why I'm here. The building is old and the supply closet is basically empty, but I spent the weekend printing worksheets at the library and laminating them myself. Cost me about forty dollars out of pocket, which I noticed when I checked my account this morning.

I set up a reading corner with pillows I found at a thrift store. It looks decent. The other second-grade teacher, Mrs. Okada, lent me a box of leveled readers she wasn't using. I mapped out the first unit already and I feel like I actually know what I'm doing for Monday. Haven't felt that way in a while. Small thing, but it matters. The commute is long and the parking lot has potholes, but I walked into that classroom and the kids were waiting and it just felt like where I was supposed to be.

**Nudge:** "Forty dollars on worksheets your first week — what made that feel worth it?"

**Response:** Because if I don't set this up right from the start, the kids lose momentum and I lose the year. I've seen it happen during student teaching. You can't get that first month back.

## Entry 1 - 2025-09-13

My old cooperating teacher from Edina called today to tell me they still have a position open. Full benefits, union contract, thirty kids max per class. She said she put in a word for me and they'd probably take me without a second interview.

I told her I'd think about it and then I sat in the parking lot for twenty minutes. Birch Lane doesn't even have a functioning copier half the time. But the literacy program here is the whole reason I came. I keep thinking about Mateo and the other kids who just started trusting me. I said no. I think I said no. I told her I'd call back Monday but I already know I'm not going to.

**Nudge:** "You sat there twenty minutes. What were you arguing with yourself about?"

**Response:** Whether I'm being stupid. Whether six months from now I'll wish I'd just taken the safe thing.

## Entry 2 - 2025-09-14

Barely slept. Kept going over that phone call. The Edina salary would have covered my student loans and left room to breathe. I know exactly what I'm giving up and I still can't make myself call her back.

## Entry 3 - 2025-09-21

Birch Lane sent around a memo about possible mid-year budget cuts. Nothing confirmed, but the newer hires would be first to go. That's me. Mrs. Okada told me not to worry, that they say this every fall, but she's been here eleven years so of course she's not worried.

Meanwhile my mom keeps forwarding me job postings from the county school district back home. Steady pay, pension, close to family. Every time she sends one I feel my chest tighten. She's not wrong — those jobs are real and they'd solve a lot of problems. But I keep picturing myself in a fluorescent classroom running a curriculum someone else wrote, and something in me just won't do it. I deleted the emails.

**Nudge:** "Your chest tightened. What does that feel like exactly?"

**Response:** Like I said, it's just stress. Everybody's stressed in September.

## Entry 4 - 2025-09-24

Had a normal day for once. Taught the short vowel unit, kids did okay on the pre-assessment. Ate lunch in the staff room instead of my classroom, which I almost never do. Talked to the PE teacher about the fall festival coming up. She asked if I wanted to run a booth and I said sure without even thinking about it. That felt easy for once.

After school I graded papers and reorganized the reading corner. Mateo finished his first leveled reader and asked if he could take the next one home. I said yes. His mom came to pick him up and thanked me for sending books home. She said he reads to his little brother at night now. That's the kind of thing that doesn't show up on any evaluation form but it's the whole point.

Drove home, made pasta, watched half a movie. Nothing remarkable. Just a day that went the way it was supposed to. Even the drive home felt calm. No knot in my stomach, no replaying conversations. Just a Tuesday.

Long week but I got through it. The reading groups are actually working. I can see the lower group catching up — two kids moved from level B to level D in three weeks. I stayed late Thursday to regroup the library baskets and it took forever, but now every kid can find a book at their level without asking me.

I'm tired but it's the right kind of tired. Like I did something that mattered today and I can feel it in my body. Haven't felt that way at a job before. Even when the copier jammed twice and I had to hand-write a worksheet on the whiteboard instead.

**Nudge:** "What does the wrong kind of tired feel like?"

**Response:** Like when I was student teaching in Edina and I'd come home drained but couldn't point to anything I'd actually built. Just following someone else's plan all day.

I know she's right about the math. I know the safer path is right there. But every time someone lays it out for me like that, something in me digs in harder. I don't want to build my life around avoiding the worst-case scenario. I want to build it around something I actually chose.

I didn't say that to her. I just said I'd look into it. But I'm not going to.

**Nudge:** "What did you actually choose when you picked Birch Lane?"

**Response:** I chose the kids. Isn't that enough?
