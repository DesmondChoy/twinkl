## Entry 2 - 2025-09-24

Double shift at the wine bar yesterday because Catarina called in sick. My shoulders are wrecked and I didn't get to the studio at all. But there was this moment around 10pm when the terrace was full, the river was doing that thing where it catches all the light from Gaia, and I poured a glass of vinho verde for a couple who were clearly on a first date. They were so nervous and sweet. I stayed a second longer than I needed to, just watching.

It's not that I love the bar job. It's repetitive and my feet hurt and the owner underpays everyone. But moments like that — small, warm, not mine exactly but near me — those are what I moved here for. Not every good thing needs to be productive. Some things are just good.

Fell asleep on the couch with the cat before I could even shower. Woke up at 4am with a stiff neck. Worth it, somehow.

## Entry 6 - 2025-10-09

Couldn't sleep last night because I kept doing the math. Rent is due next week, the gallery payment won't come until after the show closes in November, and my bar hours got cut because the owner's nephew is picking up shifts now. I have maybe three weeks of savings. Three.

So I opened my laptop and looked at design job listings in Lisbon. There are plenty. Some of them pay more than I ever made before. I could apply, interview remotely, move back by December. The rational part of my brain was already composing the CV update. And the whole time it felt like swallowing something dry.

I closed the laptop. Didn't apply. But I also didn't feel relieved — I just felt stuck. Like the choice is between going back to something that deadened me or staying here and watching the money run out. There's no version where I get to keep this life without it costing something, and tonight the cost feels too visible to ignore.

**Nudge:** "What would staying look like if money wasn't the problem?"
