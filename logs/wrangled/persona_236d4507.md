## Entry 2 - 2025-02-22

Meeting with both supervisors today and I'm still fuming a little. We were going over the chapter structure for the dissertation and I had a clear plan — three empirical chapters, the middle one built around the comparative case study I've been developing since October. Prof. Berger agreed with it in our last one-on-one. Today, Dr. Hofer pushed back hard. He wants the comparative chapter folded into an appendix and the middle section replaced with a quantitative replication study.

I argued for maybe five minutes. Laid out why the comparative design was stronger, why it fit the theoretical contribution. He kept saying "the committee will want robustness" and "you'll thank me later." Prof. Berger didn't intervene. She just sat there with her reading glasses on, looking at the outline like it was a menu.

I gave in. Said I'd draft both options and we could revisit. Which is code for: I'll do it his way because fighting him will cost me more than the chapter is worth. He's on two hiring committees I care about.

The worst part is I know the comparative chapter is better work. I know it. And I folded anyway because the politics mattered more than being right. I hate that I made that calculation so fast.
