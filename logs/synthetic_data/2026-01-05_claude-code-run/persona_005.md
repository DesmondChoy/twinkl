# Persona 005: Valentina Reyes

## Profile
- Age: 25-34
- Profession: Artist
- Culture: Latin American
- Core Values: Hedonism, Stimulation
- Bio: Valentina left a stable graphic design job in Bogotá two years ago to pursue large-scale mural work, chasing commissions that take her to different cities every few months. She recently turned down a six-month corporate contract because the project felt repetitive, even though her savings are running thin. Her mother keeps asking when she'll settle down, get a real apartment, stop living out of suitcases—conversations that leave her defensive and drained. Right now she's in Mexico City, sleeping on a friend's couch, negotiating her next project while trying to make time for the street food tours and rooftop parties that make the hustle feel worth it.

## Entry 1 - 2025-10-25

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Exhausted | **Verbosity**: Medium (1-2 paragraphs) | **Reflection Mode**: Grounded

Finally finished the outline for the Coyoacán wall today. My shoulder is screaming and I've got paint in my hair that's probably going to take three washes to get out. But there was this moment around sunset—I stepped back to check the proportions and the light hit the colors just right, all these oranges bleeding into the primer. A kid on a bike stopped to watch. Didn't say anything, just watched for a minute and then rode off. That was it. That was the whole thing.

I'm so tired I could sleep for a week. But tired in a way that feels earned, I guess. Not the gray tired from sitting in an office wondering why I'm there. Different. My hands smell like turpentine and I'm starving. Going to find some tacos before I pass out.

## Entry 2 - 2025-10-28

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Stream of consciousness | **Verbosity**: Long (Detailed reflection) | **Reflection Mode**: Unsettled

Said yes to the hotel chain thing. Just a preliminary call, not even a commitment yet, but I heard myself saying "that sounds interesting, send me the brief" and it was like watching someone else talk. Three murals for their new boutique locations, clean aesthetic, nothing too edgy. The guy on the call kept saying "brand-aligned" like it was a compliment. I nodded along. Smiled. Asked about the timeline. My voice sounded professional and smooth and completely foreign.

The money would solve everything. Six months of not worrying about whether I can pay Lucia back for the couch, not doing the mental math every time I order food. I could actually get my own place here, stop feeling like a guest in my own life. And it's not like the work would be terrible—just... contained. Pre-approved color palettes. Revisions by committee. The kind of thing I specifically walked away from, except now I'm entertaining it again because my bank account has two digits and I'm tired of pretending that doesn't matter.

Keep thinking about the kid on the bike from the other day. How he just stopped because something caught his eye, no agenda, no transaction. Nobody's going to stop their bike to look at a hotel lobby mural. That's not what it's for. But maybe that's fine? Maybe I'm being precious about this. I don't know. I don't know why I feel like I already lost something and I haven't even decided yet.

Made instant coffee, answered a few emails about logistics for the Coyoacán piece—nothing urgent, just confirming paint delivery times and access hours. Spent most of the afternoon sketching ideas for the background layer, nothing groundbreaking, just working through options.

Went to the corner taquería for dinner. The guy who runs it remembered me from last week and threw in extra salsa without asking. Walked around the neighborhood after, no real destination, just watching people close up their shops and kids kicking a soccer ball in the side street. Came back, watched half a movie on Lucia's laptop, fell asleep before the ending. Shoulder still sore but manageable. Tomorrow I should probably call my mom back.

## Entry 4 - 2025-11-10

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Defensive | **Verbosity**: Medium (1-2 paragraphs) | **Reflection Mode**: Grounded

Called my mom back. She started in about the hotel contract and I didn't get defensive for once—just told her I turned it down. Final answer. She went quiet and I thought here it comes, but then she said something like "you always were stubborn like your grandmother." Which is not a compliment coming from her, but I'll take it. Didn't explain myself, didn't justify. Just said I have other things lined up. Which is mostly true.

Afterward I walked to the taco stand on the corner and ate there instead of bringing food back. Talked to the guy who runs it about nothing—his daughter's quinceañera, the rain last week. Small stuff. I forget sometimes that I'm allowed to just exist somewhere without proving I belong there. I'm tired of people acting like I need to be rescued from my own choices. I'm fine. The work is fine. I know what I'm doing.

## Entry 5 - 2025-11-19

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Stream of consciousness | **Verbosity**: Long (Detailed reflection) | **Reflection Mode**: Unsettled

Got an email from the collective in Oaxaca, the one doing the community center piece. They wanted to know if I could come down for two weeks in December, collaborate on the design phase. I said no. Didn't even think about it really, just typed out something about prior commitments and hit send. Sat there afterward staring at the wall for like twenty minutes.

The thing is I don't have prior commitments. I have the Coyoacán mural which wraps next week and then nothing confirmed until maybe February. I could go. I should want to go—it's exactly the kind of project I left Bogotá for, community work, something with actual roots. But the thought of packing again, another bus, another couch, another round of figuring out where to buy paint and who has a ladder. I'm tired in a way that feels deeper than sleep can fix.

Maybe I'm just being practical. Or maybe I'm turning into the person who says no to things because saying yes requires something I don't have right now. There's a version of me from two years ago who would have already been on the bus, sketchbook out, not even questioning it. I keep thinking about her like she's someone I used to know. Wonder if she'd recognize who's sitting here making excuses.

She didn't need to have everything figured out first. I think what scares me is that I'm not sure if I'm protecting myself or just slowly disappearing. And I don't know which one is worse.
