## Entry 4 - 2025-12-18

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Exhausted | **Verbosity**: Long (Detailed reflection) | **Reflection Mode**: Unsettled

The board meeting went longer than expected. Four hours of slides and projections and people talking about "sustainability metrics" like they're just another line item. Which I guess they are, to them. I sat there taking notes, presenting my quarter's work, answering questions about cost-benefit ratios. The whole time I kept thinking about the river.

Not any specific river. Just—rivers. Water that flows somewhere, carries things, connects places. The Amazon, obviously, but also the smaller ones near my parents' old village. I haven't been back there in maybe five years now. My mother keeps asking. I keep saying work is too busy.

After the meeting, one of the executives pulled me aside to congratulate me on the waste reduction numbers. Said I was "making real impact." I thanked him. Shook his hand. But walking back to my office I couldn't stop thinking about the fact that next quarter they want to cut my team's budget by 15%. Real impact. I didn't push back in the meeting. I should have said something. I just nodded and wrote it down like everything else.

I haven't taken a real trip in months. The weekend excursions stopped sometime around September. I didn't notice until now. Work kept expanding to fill the space. I'm not sure how I feel about that.
