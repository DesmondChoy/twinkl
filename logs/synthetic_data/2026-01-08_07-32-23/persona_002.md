## Entry 4 - 2025-12-15

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Brief and factual | **Verbosity**: Long (Detailed reflection) | **Reflection Mode**: Grounded

Closed our seed round today. 1.2 million. Elena cried. I didn't, which surprised me. I thought I'd feel more... something. Relief, maybe, or that rush I used to get when a new project started taking shape.

Instead it was quiet. We signed the papers, shook hands with the lead investor, walked out into the December heat (still not used to Florida winters). Elena wanted to celebrate so we grabbed Cuban sandwiches from this place near the office. She kept talking about hiring plans and roadmaps and I was nodding along but my mind was somewhere else.

What I kept thinking about was my dad. He ran the same dry cleaning business for thirty-one years. Same storefront, same route to work, same lunch at the same diner every Tuesday. I used to think that was the saddest thing in the world. Now I'm wondering if he knew something I didn't. He seemed content. Actually content, not performing it. I never asked him about it. He passed in 2019 and I was in Vietnam at the time, chasing some idea I can't even remember now.
