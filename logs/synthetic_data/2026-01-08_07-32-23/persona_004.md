I keep thinking about the break room at Horizon Financial. The bad coffee, the fluorescent lights, the same conversations about weekend plans. I don't miss it. But I understood the rules there. Here, everything feels provisional.

## Entry 3 - 2025-12-12

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Emotional/Venting | **Verbosity**: Long (Detailed reflection) | **Reflection Mode**: Grounded

Sat in on a faculty meeting today as a note-taker for my advisor. Three hours of people talking past each other about curriculum changes that won't affect anyone for five years. But at one point, Dr. Reyes turned to me and asked what I thought about integrating more practitioner perspectives into the first-year courses. Actually asked. Not as a formality, but like she wanted to know.

I talked about my twelve years doing systems implementations, the gap between what textbooks describe and what actually happens when you're sitting in a conference room with a skeptical CFO and a deadline that got moved up twice. She took notes. The department chair nodded. Someone said "that's a really good point" and it wasn't sarcastic.

Walking back to my office after, I felt like I belonged there. Not just allowed to be there, but like I had something to offer that the twenty-four-year-olds straight from undergrad don't have yet. Janet asked how my day went and I actually had something to tell her. Lily asked if I was a teacher now and I said "sort of, maybe, eventually." That felt true.

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Emotional/Venting | **Verbosity**: Medium (1-2 paragraphs) | **Reflection Mode**: Grounded

My old manager Dave called today. First time in eight months. He's still at Horizon, still running the same department, still complaining about the same vendor they've been trying to replace since I was there. He wanted to catch up, he said, but really he wanted to complain to someone who'd understand.

I listened for forty minutes. Nodded along to problems I used to carry home with me every night. And when he asked how the PhD was going, I told him about the faculty meeting. About Dr. Reyes asking my opinion. He got quiet for a second, then said "that's great, man" in that tone people use when they don't quite believe what you're describing is real. I think he was jealous. That surprised me.

## Entry 5 - 2025-12-19

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Self-reflective | **Verbosity**: Long (Detailed reflection) | **Reflection Mode**: Unsettled

Got the funding letter today. My research assistantship is guaranteed through next fall, but after that it says "contingent on satisfactory progress and available departmental funds." I've read that sentence maybe fifteen times now. Everyone says it's standard language, boilerplate, nothing to worry about. My advisor said the same thing when I asked her about it.

But I keep thinking about what "contingent" means. Contingent on what, exactly? On grant cycles I don't control? On some committee's assessment of what counts as satisfactory? At Horizon, I had a contract. Annual reviews had forms and checkboxes and if you met the numbers, you knew where you stood. This is different. This is "we'll let you know."

Janet found me staring at the letter at 11pm and told me to come to bed. She didn't say I was being ridiculous, which I appreciated. She just said "you'll figure it out" and turned off the kitchen light. I want to believe that. But I also want something signed, something with dates and numbers, something that doesn't have the word "contingent" in it. I don't know how everyone else seems fine with this.
