Went to the studio early. Needed to just paint without thinking about schedules or compromises. Worked on the largest canvas for the show -- the one based on the view from the mill bridge. It's the same view Grand-mere painted in 1971, and Maman in 1994, and now me. Three versions of the same bend in the river, each a little different because the trees change and the banks shift, but the bones of it stay.

A friend who paints digitally told me last week that I should try working on a tablet. Faster, cheaper, infinite undo. I told him I like the smell of linseed oil and the resistance of the canvas. He laughed like I was joking. I wasn't. The materials matter. The process matters. You don't inherit a craft by switching to whatever's newest. You inherit it by keeping your hands in the same medium.
