I sat in a parking lot for five minutes just breathing before I could drive again.

## Entry 2 - 2025-12-09

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Self-reflective | **Verbosity**: Long (Detailed reflection) | **Reflection Mode**: Unsettled

Been thinking a lot about what I'm doing here. Eight days since that parking lot moment and things haven't really changed, but somehow I feel different about them. Maybe it's because I finally told my mother about the lunch thing. Not directly, but she figured it out when I hesitated too long on the video call. She cried. That was worse than anything.

I keep telling myself this is temporary. Two years, maybe three, and I'll have enough saved for a proper flat where they can all stay. My father could see the specialist here, not the overworked clinic doctor back home. Noor could finish her degree somewhere with more opportunities. That's the plan. But lately I wonder if I'm lying to myself the way I lie to them about lunch.

The other drivers at the charging station talk about going home. Some of them have been saying "two more years" for five years. One guy, Faisal, told me he missed his daughter's first steps, her first words, her first day of school. He showed me photos and his hands were shaking. I don't have kids, but I think about Noor's graduation next spring. I won't be there. I'll be here, driving someone to the airport or the mall or wherever, while my sister walks across a stage and my parents clap and there's an empty chair where I should be.

I sent extra money home this week instead of buying new sandals. The sole of my left one is coming apart. It's fine for now. Everything is fine for now. That's what I keep telling myself.

## Entry 3 - 2025-12-16

### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Stream of consciousness | **Verbosity**: Medium (1-2 paragraphs) | **Reflection Mode**: Neutral

Strange day. Good strange, I think? One of my regulars, Mrs. Al-Farsi, she takes rides to the hospital every Tuesday and Thursday for her treatments. Today she asked me to wait. Normally I can't because waiting is lost money but something about how she asked. So I waited. Forty-five minutes in the parking structure reading news on my phone and calculating what I was losing.

When she came out she looked smaller somehow. She paid me double and I tried to refuse but she put her hand on mine and said in Arabic, "Let someone take care of you for once, son." I drove her home and she invited me in for tea and suddenly I'm sitting in her kitchen eating date cookies while she tells me about her late husband and how he used to drive a taxi in Muscat. She gave me a box of cookies to take home. I ate them for dinner. Best meal I've had in weeks, not because of the food, just because of all of it. Faisal asked why I was smiling at the charging station later and I didn't know how to explain.
