Got the email this afternoon. I'm in. New Mexico residency, three months starting in March. I read the acceptance twice and then I just sat there staring at my screen for a while. Not jumping up and down, not immediately texting everyone I know. Just this weird quiet feeling, like something clicking into place.

Called my mom to tell her. She's worried about me being alone in the desert, which is funny because I'm alone in my studio most of the time anyway. But she's excited too. She asked if I was going to use this to "focus on one style finally" and I managed not to sigh into the phone. I told her I'm going to use it to make the best work I've ever made, whatever form that takes.

Started planning what to bring. Limited luggage means I have to choose which materials matter most. I'm thinking oil paints, my good brushes, sketchbooks, and nothing else. Let the desert fill in the gaps. Maybe that's what I've been missing, not a signature style but just enough constraint to push against. Every time I've made something I was proud of, it was because I had a problem to solve, not unlimited options. Something to think about.

My success rate for applications this year ended at nineteen percent. Still not where I want to be. But for once the math feels less important than what's actually happening. I'm going to make art in the desert. That's real.
