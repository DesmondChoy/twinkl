## Entry 6 - 2025-10-20
### Initial Entry
**Tone**: Self-reflective | **Verbosity**: Long (Detailed reflection) | **Reflection Mode**: Neutral

I did something small today that I keep thinking about. I was in the studio early, nobody else there yet, and I pulled out a fresh sheet and started writing the bismillah in naskh the way jiddo taught me. Perfect form, clean lines. Then without really deciding to, I took a second sheet and wrote it again but let the letters drift apart, stretched the connections, let the lam curve wider than it should. It still read as the phrase. Just looser.

I stared at both sheets for a long time. The second one wasn't ugly. It wasn't disrespectful. It was the same words with more air in them. Teta could still read it. Jiddo would have recognized the hand.

I don't know what this means for my thesis. I rolled both sheets up and put them in my bag. I haven't shown anyone. Part of me thinks if I show Professor Mansour she'll want me to go further, push harder, and I'll end up somewhere I didn't agree to. And part of me thinks if I show Teta she'll look at the second sheet and ask why I'm being careless. So for now they're mine. Two versions of the same prayer, sitting in my bag, and I'm the only one who knows about both.
